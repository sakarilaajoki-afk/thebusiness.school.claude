In one lesson

Running it is three steps

1

Start a session

Log in, set each phase length, and get a 6-digit PIN. Put the big-screen leaderboard on the projector with one click.

2

Students join

They open the link on any device, type the PIN, and up to eight teams form automatically — every firm gets a CEO.

3

You steer, it scores

Pace the phases, trigger an event, watch the live board. At the end: automatic grades, reports and certificates.

After every session

Every student gets personal feedback

When the simulation ends, each student receives an AI-generated report based on their actual decisions — not a generic score. Plus a printable certificate with their grade.

Strength

What they did well

Specific decisions where the student excelled — pricing moves, negotiation wins, timely VAT filings or smart investments.

Focus

One area to improve

A single, focused insight tied to an actual choice they made. Concrete and actionable — never a generic critique.

Momentum

Encouragement to keep going

Personalised motivation that reflects their journey, written to build confidence and curiosity for the next session.

Questions

What teachers ask first

Is it free for teachers?＋

The teacher demo is free — no credit card and no student accounts. Licensing is per school (or per MAT), per year, with a free classroom pilot before any commitment.

Which specifications does it map to?＋

Every decision is tagged to the Edexcel A-Level Business specification (9BS0), shown two ways: a plain theme label for students and the exact sub-code for teachers. It also works as an applied activity for AQA (7132 / 8132), BTEC and IGCSE Business. The full mapping is on the Curriculum page.

Do students need to install anything or make accounts?＋

No. It runs in any browser. Students open the link and join with a 6-digit PIN — nothing to download, no logins to manage.

How many students, and how long does it take?＋

Up to 32 students in up to 8 teams. Length is flexible — you set a timer for each of the three phases, so a session can fit a short lesson or run longer, and phases can auto-advance so it never stalls.

What does the teacher see and control?＋

A live control panel: start and pace the phases, trigger events, watch firm standings, and put a big-screen leaderboard on the projector. Afterwards you get automatic grades, student and class reports, and certificates.
